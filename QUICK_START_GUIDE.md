# Quick Start Guide - Hourglass Funnel & Execution Prioritization

## 🚀 Getting Started (5 Minutes)

### Step 1: Select Hourglass Funnel (1 min)
1. Open **Dashboard**
2. Look for **Funnel Selector** in the toolbar (next to Objectives filter)
3. Click dropdown → Select **"Hourglass"**
4. Dashboard automatically updates to show 7-stage view

### Step 2: Add a Prioritized Idea (2 min)
1. Go to **Ideas** page
2. Click **"+ Novo Ideia"**
3. Fill in basic info:
   - **Title:** "Optimize landing page CTA"
   - **Description:** "Test new button placement and color"
   - **Category:** "Conversion"
4. **NEW FIELDS:**
   - **Impact:** Select **"High"** (this could improve conversions significantly)
   - **Effort:** Select **"Low"** (just a page edit)
5. Click **"Criar"**

**Result:** Idea gets Priority **Tier A** (red badge) with recommendation "Execute immediately"

### Step 3: Check "What to Do Next" (1 min)
1. Go back to **Dashboard**
2. Scroll down to see **"What to Do Next"** widget (section 3.5)
3. Your new idea appears in red as **Tier A** item
4. It shows: impact (H), effort (L), and why it's a quick win

### Step 4: Check Hourglass Stages (1 min)
1. Still in Dashboard with Hourglass selected
2. Look at **Objective cards** → they now show 7 stages instead of 5
3. See metrics for each stage:
   - **Awareness:** Impressions, reach, brand searches
   - **Interest:** Clicks, sessions, engagement
   - **Consideration:** Leads, demos, CTR
   - **Conversion:** Revenue, conversions, ROI
   - **Retention:** Churn, NPS, repeat visits
   - **Expansion:** MRR/ARR growth
   - **Advocacy:** Referrals, followers, NPS

## 🎯 Priority Tier Quick Reference

### Tier A (Red) - Execute Immediately
- **Formula:** Impact High, Effort Low or Medium
- **What it means:** Quick wins that move the needle
- **Time to execute:** This week
- **Example:** "Improve CTA button color" (high impact, low effort)

### Tier B (Orange) - Schedule Soon
- **Formula:** Impact High, Effort High OR Impact Medium, Effort Low
- **What it means:** Good ROI but requires more work, or easy with medium benefit
- **Time to execute:** This month
- **Example:** "Implement email nurturing" (medium impact, low effort)

### Tier C (Blue) - Plan for Medium-Term
- **Formula:** Impact Medium, Effort Medium or High, OR Impact Low, Effort Low
- **What it means:** Worth doing but not urgent
- **Time to execute:** Next quarter
- **Example:** "Redesign landing page" (high impact but high effort)

### Tier D (Gray) - Deprioritize
- **Formula:** Impact Low, Effort High or Medium
- **What it means:** Low return for effort, skip for now
- **Time to execute:** Only if time/budget available
- **Example:** "Rebrand marketing collateral" (low impact, high effort)

## 🔍 Understanding the Hourglass Stages

### Awareness Stage
**You see:** Impressions, reach, brand searches, followers
**It tells you:** How visible is your brand?
**Optimize:** Add ads, content marketing, PR
**Red flag:** Very low impressions despite big budget

### Interest Stage
**You see:** Clicks, sessions, engagement metrics
**It tells you:** What % of aware people engage?
**Optimize:** Test creatives, landing pages, messaging
**Red flag:** Lots of impressions but few clicks (bad creative)

### Consideration Stage
**You see:** Leads, form submissions, demos, trials
**It tells you:** How many interested people become prospects?
**Optimize:** Improve value proposition, lead magnets, demos
**Red flag:** High clicks but low lead generation

### Conversion Stage
**You see:** Revenue, conversions, deals closed, ROI
**It tells you:** Are we making money?
**Optimize:** Improve sales process, offer, closing rate
**Red flag:** Leads available but low conversion rate

### Retention Stage
**You see:** Churn rate, NPS, repeat visits, engagement
**It tells you:** Do customers stay and use the product?
**Optimize:** Customer success, product improvements, support
**Red flag:** Good acquisition but high churn

### Expansion Stage
**You see:** MRR/ARR growth from existing customers
**It tells you:** How much are existing customers growing?
**Optimize:** Upsells, cross-sells, higher tiers
**Red flag:** Flat expansion while revenue stalls

### Advocacy Stage
**You see:** NPS promoters, referrals, followers, shares
**It tells you:** Are customers promoting us?
**Optimize:** Build community, referral programs, events
**Red flag:** Happy customers but not referring

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Tip 1: Use Hourglass + Prioritization Together
- Assign ideas/experiments to Hourglass stages
- Prioritize each by impact/effort
- See "What to Do Next" showing best next actions per stage
- Execute in order: A → B → C → D

### Tip 2: Monitor Stage Transitions
- Watch the ratio between stages
- Example: 1000 impressions → 100 clicks → 10 leads → 1 conversion
- If a stage has low conversion rate, focus optimizations there
- Use Insights to identify bottlenecks per stage

### Tip 3: Plan Initiatives by Stage
- In Plan page, assign each initiative to its target stage
- View roadmap by customer lifecycle
- Ensure you're investing across all stages
- Don't over-invest in awareness if conversion is the bottleneck

### Tip 4: Filter Analysis by Funnel Model
- Switch between Hourglass and other models to see different perspectives
- AIDA: Simpler 4-stage view
- AARRR: SaaS-focused 5-stage view
- Hourglass: Complete 7-stage lifecycle
- Use whichever matches your business model

## ❓ Common Questions

**Q: Should I use Hourglass for my business?**
A: Yes if you want to track the complete customer lifecycle including retention and expansion. Use AARRR instead if you're pure-play SaaS focused on activation and retention metrics.

**Q: How do I know if Impact is High or Medium?**
A: Ask: "If we nail this, by how much does a key metric improve?"
- 30%+ improvement → High
- 10-30% improvement → Medium
- <10% improvement → Low

**Q: What if a task is High Impact but High Effort?**
A: It's Tier C - worth doing but not urgent. Build capacity for it, don't attempt under time pressure.

**Q: Can I switch funnels without losing data?**
A: Yes! All your data stays. The funnel selector just regroups the same metrics into different stages.

**Q: When does the "Expansion" stage data appear?**
A: When you connect your CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot) - coming soon. For now, manually track MRR/ARR growth.
